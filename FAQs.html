1. What is health insurance ?

Health insurance is an insurance product that provides cover for the medical and surgical expenses of an insured person, in case of a medical emergency. However, you are required to pay a premium to avail health insurance policy.

2. Why should I buy health insurance ?

You should purchase health insurance so that you don’t lose your lifelong savings while paying for medical bills in a critical situation.

buying my health insurance policy ?

4. What are the types of health insurance plans I can buy ?

You can buy Individual Health Insurance Plans, Family Floater Health Insurance Plans, Senior Citizen Health Insurance Plans, Critical Illness Insurance Plans, Maternity Health Insurance Plans, Group Health Insurance Plans, and Personal Accident Insurance Plans.

5. What is the eligible age to buy health insurance ?

You can buy Individual Health Insurance Plans, Family Floater Health Insurance Plans, Senior Citizen Health Insurance Plans, Critical Illness Insurance Plans, Maternity Health Insurance Plans, Group Health Insurance Plans, and Personal Accident Insurance Plans.

6. Will I be allowed to buy more than one health insurance plan ?

Yes, you are free to buy another plan based on your specific medical needs.

7. What are the common coverage benefits under my health insurance ?

Health insurance benefits differ from policy to policy. However, basic health insurance benefits include cover for inpatient hospitalization, pre & post-hospitalization, daycare procedures, emergency ambulance expenses, organ donor expenses, domiciliary hospitalization, OPD expenses, and more.

8. If I want to cancel my health insurance policy after purchasing it ?

If willing to cancel your health insurance policy after its purchase, you can do so within 15 days of receiving the policy documents. You will receive a refund only if you did not make any claims in the policy during that year.

9. What documents do I need if I want to buy health insurance ?

If you want to buy health insurance, you need to submit the documents like an identity card, driving license, and pre-medical check-up report (in some cases). The documents are required for age proof, identity proof, address proof, and medical check-ups.

10. Can I pay my health insurance premium in installments ?

In general, the health insurance premium is paid every year. But, you can pay your premium in installments (monthly, quarterly, or half-yearly basis) as well.

11. Will my health insurance policy expire if not renewed on time ?

Yes. Every policy comes with a validity of 1 year, 2 years, or 3 years, after which it needs to be renewed. The insurance companies provide you a grace period of 15 days from the date of expiry during which you can pay the premium and continue availing of policy benefits. If the premium is not paid even during the grace period, then the policy will lapse, and you will lose the accumulated benefits such as waiting for period benefits or No Claim Bonus and will have to buy a new policy to seek protection from healthcare expenses.
